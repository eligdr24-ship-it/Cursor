MERIDIAN · MDRN

Meridian Process Systems

Mission-critical process control software for regulated industrial plants

72

Conviction
0–100

Would this deserve 1 of 20 portfolio seats for 15 years?

Conditional yes — quality is high; valuation gate is the constraint. Preview data only.

Executive summary

Meridian sells plant-level control software that sits between sensors and operators in chemical, pharma, and food plants. Switching costs are high once validated; revenue is ~78% recurring. The market still prices it like a cyclical industrial toolkit rather than a sticky software layer with expanding attach rates.

Investment thesis

If Meridian continues converting installed hardware relationships into multi-module SaaS while keeping churn below 4%, free-cash-flow compounding can re-rate the business from “industrial supplier” to “regulated workflow platform” over a decade.

Business overview

Founded as an instrumentation integrator; today ~62% of revenue is software subscriptions, 23% professional services, 15% legacy hardware. Customers are mid-to-large plants where downtime and regulatory failure are existential costs. Average contract life 6+ years.

Competitive position

  • High switching costs after plant validation
  • Regulatory documentation moat (audit trails)
  • Distribution via installed base + OEMs
  • Weakness: hyperscale cloud vendors adjacent

Management & capital

  • CEO owns ~9% economically
  • Buybacks only after organic reinvestment
  • No empire-building M&A in last 5 years
  • Comp tied to FCF/share, not revenue

Hidden assets

  • Validated plant telemetry archive (rare training set for process AI)
  • OEM certification network that new entrants cannot shortcut
  • Under-monetized modules already deployed but unused (~30% attach gap)
  • Potential services carve-out if software multiple expands

Risks

  • Cloud platform vendors bundle adjacent monitoring
  • Customer concentration in chemicals cycle
  • Regulatory change that commoditizes audit features
  • Key-person risk in domain engineering talent

Scenarios (10–15 year)

Bull · 25%

Attach rates hit 70%, AI process layer becomes standard, FCF grows ~18% CAGR. Multiple expands.

Base · 50%

Steady share gains, mid-teens FCF growth, modest re-rating as mix shifts to software.

Bear · 25%

Platform encroachment + cyclical pause; growth slows to high-single digits, multiple compresses.

Valuation assessment

At ~28× NTM FCF, the stock embeds solid execution but not a full platform outcome. Upside exists if mix and attach rates surprise; limited margin of safety if growth stalls. Gate status: yellow — acceptable only with position sizing discipline.

Monitor next

  • Net revenue retention
  • Module attach rate
  • Logo churn
  • FCF conversion
  • Competitive win/loss vs cloud suites

What would change our mind

Upgrade: sustained NRR >115% with rising AI attach and no share loss to platforms.
Kill: structural churn above 8%, or clear evidence customers prefer bundled hyperscaler stacks.

Suggested upgrades to your methodology

  1. Split scores: Business quality vs valuation as separate axes — never blend into one vanity number.
  2. Evidence citations: Every claim must link to a filing, transcript, or primary source.
  3. Moat trajectory: Score expanding / stable / eroding — static moats decay silently.
  4. Steelman bear first: Write the kill case before the bull case to reduce confirmation bias.
  5. Probability-weighted scenarios: Require explicit % weights that sum to 100.
  6. Portfolio scarcity test: Rank vs current 20 seats — absolute “good” is not enough.
  7. Living thesis journal: Quarterly check against kill criteria; auto-flag drift.
